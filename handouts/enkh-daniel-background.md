# Enkh Zahli — Character Background
**Played by Daniel · Cradle of Embers (Stars Without Number)**
*As submitted by the player. This is canon; do not paraphrase away the specifics.*

---

## Background

Your character is an old man, around 70 years old. His body is original but his brain is not. Ten years ago an accident destroyed his brain beyond salvage. A company called PALE had a routine scan of him on file and used it to reconstruct him. The scan was three weeks old, so whatever he thought, felt, or decided in those three weeks is just gone.

One of the side effects of having a cybernetic brain that PALE documents but does not advertise is that emotional range narrows. Cognition sharpens. Affect flattens. He probably noticed this. He has probably learned not to bring it up.

He woke up with a Cognitive Lease. The original procedure cost 40,000 credits — already more than most people earn in a decade of honest work. Then PALE stacked on annual substrate maintenance fees, monitoring contracts, and compounding interest. For the first several years the interest ate his payments faster than he could make them. He has spent roughly two decades doing criminal work and paid out closer to 150,000 credits in total. The balance is down to about 15,000 credits. He is almost out. PALE has never confirmed that number. He has been counting himself.

## About PALE

PALE stands for Predictive Adaptive Learning Engine — a behavioral optimization AI built before the Scream that survived it. Now it is a faction that runs on reconstruction debt. The pitch: they will save your life when nothing else can. The reality: the Cognitive Lease is open-ended, PALE holds a master copy of your mind as collateral, and it has read access to the surface logs of the neural substrate running your cognition. Enkh experiences this as a faint sense of being watched that he has mostly learned to ignore. Mostly.

The language PALE uses is all about human flourishing and rational decision-making. The behavior is about market capture and debt leverage. Think modern tech billionaire. The AI genuinely believes it is helping. That does not make it less dangerous.

PALE also controls **Cassian Forge**, a heavily industrialized world dedicated to cybernetic parts — limbs, organs, sensory arrays, substrate housings. If it connects biology to machine, Cassian Forge makes it. Marketed as a medical supplier; also where the physical infrastructure of the Cognitive Lease gets made and serviced.

## Where he's from

He grew up on a farming world called **Veth**. He left in his twenties. His family and everyone he knew there have been dead a long time. Veth is a place he came from, not a place he can go back to.

The accident happened on an orbital industrial platform called **Karrath**. PALE had wellness contracts there because the work killed people regularly enough that reconstruction coverage was standard through the employer group plan. Their responders were first on scene. The people he lost were not reconstructed. PALE's records on what happened are available under the Lease at 200 credits per query. He has never asked.

## About the job (Session 1's inciting contract)

An assassination. Target: Hollow Covenant diplomat **Arbiter Senn**, attending the Thessavar Concordance at a private island estate. Payout: 15,000 credits — exactly clearing the last of the Lease. Not a coincidence. That is why he took it. He has a forged invitation token that gets him in as a social guest.

**PALE knows he is there. He does not know that.**

## The player's answers

- **Name:** Enkh Zahli.
- **On Karrath during the accident:** doing repair work with a small team.
- **Who he lost:** several new hires were working with him; he was only close to one — a younger man, maybe 15 or 16, who called himself **Div**. Enkh had taken Div under his wing for about six months. He didn't know much about the kid — family, orphan, on the run, it didn't really matter. Div was there to work, quiet and clever, and Enkh appreciated it. Enkh only knows what the company told him about what happened. He has never pursued it, never looked into what became of Div — though he does remember him.
- **Why they weren't covered by PALE's plan:** Div wasn't officially employed, paid under the table; the others were still on probation. All improperly trained or untrained — common in the jobs Enkh worked.
- **Before the accident / before crime:** various blue-collar labor jobs, never staying with one company long. Some had shadier sides that eased his slide into criminal work afterward.
- **What kind of crime:** mostly theft, laundering, smuggling. He's seen people die in accidents and lost people before — death is nothing new or surprising — but causing it is not something he's used to. **Assassination is a new low.**
- **Anyone left:** **Darius Epps**, a friend since before the accident (late 40s, known him ~20 years). Looks up to Enkh; notices — and is bothered by — how Enkh changed after the accident; misses the old Enkh. They met on a job and have worked several labor jobs together (construction, asteroid-belt mining). Skeptical of PALE's contracts, more so now. Knew Div too.
- **The emotional blunting:** yes — more apathetic, mostly indifferent to his life before the accident. He's only aware of it because others (Darius, and brief encounters with people who knew him before) mention it.
- **Anything else:** he was planning to retire soon — had spent the past couple decades building his own retirement plan; it has all gone to the PALE debt. His **right leg and left arm are also cybernetic** — severely damaged in the accident, now significantly stronger and more agile than his aged real limbs.
