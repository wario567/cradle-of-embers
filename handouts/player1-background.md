## Who you are

You are old. Eighty, give or take. Your body is the original — hands that shake slightly in the cold, the weight of decades in how you move. Your brain is not original. Some years ago, an accident destroyed it beyond salvage. A company called PALE — the Predictive Adaptive Learning Engine, a faction that sells neural reconstruction the way other companies sell life insurance — had a scan of you on file from a routine check-in. They rebuilt you from it. The scan was three weeks old. Whatever you thought, felt, or decided in those three weeks is gone.

You woke up with a Cognitive Lease: an open-ended debt to PALE for the cost of your reconstruction. They hold the master copy of your mind. You've been working criminal jobs to pay it down. You don't know exactly how much is left. PALE doesn't tell you.

---

## Where you're from

You grew up on **Veth** — a quiet agricultural world, temperate and unremarkable. You left in your twenties. Everyone you knew there is long dead. Veth is a place you came from, not a place you can return to.

---

## The accident

It happened at **Karrath Platform** — an orbital industrial facility. PALE had wellness contracts there; the work killed people regularly enough that reconstruction coverage was standard issue. When the accident destroyed your brain, their responders were first on scene.

The people you lost in the accident were not reconstructed. PALE's records on what happened are available under your Lease terms at 200 credits per query. You have not asked.
