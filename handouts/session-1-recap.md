# Session 1 Recap — The Still Gardens
**Cradle of Embers · Stars Without Number**
*Thessavar — an ocean world of floating cities, drowned mountains, and something very old beneath the waves.*

---

## Cold Open — *Thessavar, five days ago*

In the black water far below the surface, a lone diver reaches out and touches a wall covered in alien hieroglyphs. The instant his hand makes contact, something like an electric shock jolts through him. He bolts for the surface — and as he rises, a second diver emerges from the shadows behind him, watching.

The first diver was **Tarik**.

---

## Thessavar — the days before

- **West** and **Ankh** pass each other in swan boats on the waterways — strangers, meeting eyes for a single moment before drifting apart.
- **West**, a Kael-7 security clone, in training on the orbital platform where the clone lines are raised: a commander reprimands the unit for breaking protocol. Falling in line is not a choice for a Kael-7. It is how the body works.
- In **Kaeldrift**, at **Veronica's bar**, **Ankh** meets his contact **Darius** and finalizes the plan: the assassination of **Arbiter Senn**.

---

## The Concordance at the Still Gardens

Arbiter Senn is set to speak at the Still Gardens, the private garden island south of Kaeldrift. West and two fellow clones are assigned to secure the area — pointedly ordered to stay **off the stage**. Before the speech, Tarik finds West and hands over a **parcel for Arbiter Senn**: a letter, and a **locket**.

Ankh steps off a swan boat, Darius in his ear. He orders a beer from Veronica — he recognizes her from the bar in Kaeldrift, but says nothing. Then he slips away. West is scanning the crowd, but Ankh wins the contest of stealth against West's watchful eye and vanishes into a hiding spot, laser pistol drawn, sights settling on Arbiter Senn.

He never fires.

## The Still Gardens fall

An **explosion of water** erupts from beneath the stage where Senn is speaking. Sirens. The Still Gardens begin to **crumble and sink**, water flooding in from every side. Ankh and West both scramble onto what's left of the speaking platform — and both know, without needing a body, that **Arbiter Senn is dead**. No human survives that.

The two of them help survivors up onto the platform as the island goes down. Then **Veronica** arrives, trailing a line of swan boats, and evacuates everyone off the drowning island.

On the boat parade back to Kaeldrift:

- A **second eruption** bursts from the island's highest point — where Senn had been speaking — followed by another siren and an **EMP pulse**. The pulse silences the noise in Ankh's mind and kills his comm line to Darius. (His cybernetic limbs, mercifully, keep working.)
- West notices the parcel from Tarik has grown **warm**.
- One of West's fellow clones was killed by debris in the initial explosion. One survives, and stays at West's side.

## Kaeldrift — "It was fun while it lasted"

Back in the city, Veronica singles out West and Ankh: *having witnessed what happened at the Still Gardens, you are in great danger. Get off-planet.* She offers safe passage on her ship, departing in under four hours — meet her at the bar.

While waiting at the bar:

- West slips away from the surviving clone and opens the parcel. The **letter**, from Tarik to Senn, makes no sense to them. The **locket** radiates warmth — and near it, West feels an uncanny sense of *rightness*, of completeness. West puts it away **without touching it**, and tells no one. Not even the clone.
- West questions Veronica about Tarik. She says she doesn't know him — but West can tell **she is lying**.
- Asking around the bar, West learns that **Senn and Tarik met here several times**. They were known in this place.
- The surviving clone decides to go search for Tarik — their handler — and convinces West to stay behind: *if it matters that one of us survives, it should be you.*
- A street urchin appears, wordlessly recognizing West as the person he was sent to find, and delivers a note: **"Found Tarik. Leave now. Not safe."**

A messenger from Veronica arrives: it's time. At the shipyard, as they board, Veronica steps onto the dock, takes one last look at the fallen leaves of Kaeldrift, and says:

> *"It was fun while it lasted."*

## Open questions going into Session 2

- What did Tarik touch down there — and who was the second diver?
- What is the locket, and why does it feel *right* to be near it?
- What does Tarik's letter to Senn mean?
- Who — or what — destroyed the Still Gardens? Was Senn the target?
- Why is Veronica lying about knowing Tarik? And where is she taking you?
- Who found Tarik, and why isn't it safe?
- Will West's clone sibling make it off Thessavar?
