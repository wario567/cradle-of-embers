# Session 2 Recap — Twelve Days in the Black
**Cradle of Embers · Stars Without Number**

---

## In the Black

The Duskline runs dark and quiet, twelve days out from a drowned world. Thessavar shrinks to a blue point behind you; Belum Freeport waits somewhere ahead. For the first time since the island went under, there is nothing to do but breathe — and learn the ship.

## The Crew

The Duskline is crewed by Driftborn — spacers in soft, worn, beige linen, people whose whole lives fit inside a hull. Captain **Mira Calder** runs the ship; her crew stays with it, half a dozen or more, and they fold passengers into the work without ceremony.

**Enkh** and **Darius** pitched in the way people do who have worked ships before — old hands, comfortable in a cargo hold. **West** helped because helping is what West does: no order required, they simply fell in. The crew noticed. Word got around. By the end of the voyage the Driftborn had quietly extended something rare to the three of them: *support*. You worked; you're good with us.

Among the crew, Enkh met **Asha Kei** — a boy of maybe fourteen or fifteen, quick, watchful, with an accent that sounds like it came off a dock somewhere far away. Something about the kid stuck with Enkh. He couldn't say what.

West, always running quiet threat assessment, noticed something else: **Mira Calder's first mate carries a tattoo on his inner forearm** — a wave breaking against a bar, with a flame inside it. The same style of ink as the crowd at Veronika's bar back in Kaeldrift.

## The Locket

All voyage long, the locket in West's pocket grew warmer. West finally worked out the pattern: **it reacts to Enkh.** Warmest when he's near.

And something else — harder to put into words. Since the first time West touched it (that little electric *zap*, like being noticed by something), the constant background anxiety West has always carried — the trepidation before every step, the checking, the doubt — has gone quiet. In its place: a feeling of *rightness*. Like they can't put a foot wrong. When West is in direct contact with the locket, the feeling magnifies tenfold.

It feels wonderful. It probably should be frightening.

## Veronika Breaks — a Little

Mid-voyage, shouting from the crew quarters. West, Enkh, and Darius went to look — and found **Veronika**, for the first time, burned down to raw emotion. Furious, then drowning in sorrow.

Someone named **Red** died back on Thessavar. One of her best. She didn't explain who he was, or what he was doing, or where "down there" is. Just:

> *"I told him not to go. He didn't listen. Now he's dead. Fuckin' idiot."*

She claims she doesn't know what happened at the Still Gardens. Both of you have a strong feeling — a certainty, really — that **she knows more than she's saying.** Nobody pushed. Not yet.

## The Message

Enkh did what he does after every job: wrote to his broker. A coded message to **Marn Coyle** — whose sector-wide chain of pawn shops fronts a quieter business — reporting the mission complete.

Messages don't transmit inside metaspace. The moment the Duskline dropped back into normal space, the message went out — and somewhere on Belum, a professionally bland man read it, checked something twice, and stopped checking.

## Where It Stands — Three Doors at Belum

The Duskline makes port at **Belum Freeport**. Three things are waiting:

1. **Veronika's ask** — help her move cargo off the ship to a warehouse in Belum. She hasn't said what it is.
2. **Marn Coyle** — his nearest Belum storefront. Payment owed. And maybe new work.
3. **The nuns** — sisters of the Hollow Covenant approached, led by one **Sister Halex**, asking for help. What they want, and why it matters, waits for next session.

## Open questions going into Session 3

- What is Veronika not saying about the Still Gardens — and who was Red to her?
- What is the locket actually *doing* to West? Why does it react to Enkh? And what registered West with that little zap?
- Will Coyle pay? 15,000 credits — the exact balance of a certain debt — is waiting to be collected. What could go wrong?
- What's in Veronika's cargo?
- What do the sisters of the Covenant want with you?
- And who else, in a port full of strangers, knows the Duskline's passenger manifest?
