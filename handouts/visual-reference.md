## THE PLAYER CHARACTERS

### Enkh Zahli
*Aging cyborg criminal, played by Daniel.*

> A weathered man of seventy, standing with the stillness of someone who has learned to conserve movement. His real hand, spotted with age, trembles faintly at rest. His right leg and left arm are visibly cybernetic — matte gunmetal plating at the joints, slightly too smooth and too strong for the rest of his aging body, a mismatch that reads as wrongness rather than augmentation. His face is unreadable: flattened affect, sharp eyes that miss nothing, the calm of a man who feels less than he used to. He is dressed for a retirement he never got to take — a faded Hawaiian shirt, worn shorts, sandals — clothing that belongs to a life he was planning before the debt ate it. A thin scar traces the base of his skull, just visible above the collar: the substrate case seam.

### West (K7G2-87459-WST)
*Kael-7 Grade 2 security clone, they/them, played by Kisa.*

> A young adult clone, eighteen months old but built with the physical presence of a trained soldier in their mid-twenties — Grade 2 Security lineage, athletic, poised, unnervingly still when at rest. Grey-toned tactical security uniform, unadorned, functional, built for close-protection work rather than combat. Their face is calm and alert at once, trained to read a room before it reads them — the specific vigilance of someone always running threat assessment. No visible cybernetics; the compulsion that governs them lives in genetics, not hardware. Something in their eyes suggests a person still discovering they are allowed to want things.

## SESSION 1 CAST — THE STILL GARDENS

### Arbiter Senn
*Hollow Covenant diplomat, deceased.*

> A woman in her fifties, severe and immaculate, dressed in ceremonial white robes cut with sharp formal lines — Covenant diplomatic dress that reads like mourning worn in reverse. Hair pulled back with surgical precision, minimal jewelry, a posture that has never once needed to ask for attention. Cold, composed, radiating the specific untouchability of someone who has never had to acknowledge the people protecting her. She is boarding a white gondola boat on dark water, unaware this is the last hour of her life.

### Requisition Agent Tarek
*Progenitor Combine field agent, missing.*

> Broad-shouldered man in his forties, dressed exactly right for whatever room he's in — expensive but understated, the practiced polish of a professional who sells trust for a living. Warm brown eyes, an easy smile that feels genuine even though you know it's a tool. He is underwater in a diving suit, dive-light cutting a small cone through absolute black, one gloved hand reaching toward an alien wall of glowing hieroglyphs that predate humanity.

### Veronika, "the Saint"
*Kaeldrift bartender; secret leader of the River Below.*

> A woman in her mid-thirties with dark hair pulled back in a practical clip, hands roughened by years of service work — pouring drinks, hauling crates, both. She has the specific calm of someone who has already decided whether to trust you. Working clothes, nothing performative, a scar or two that don't get explained. In one image: standing at the prow of a swan boat amid chaos and flame, radiating absolute unshaken authority. In another: alone at the edge of a dock, leaves drifting down around her feet, her composure finally cracking at the corners.

### Darius Epps
*Enkh's oldest friend, criminal associate.*

A beat-up canvas work jacket that's clearly survived twenty years of construction sites and asteroid-belt mining rigs, a flat cap or worn ballcap, hands like a man who's swung a wrench more often than a gun. He carries himself like he's always slightly annoyed and completely loyal at the same time. Currently: leaning on a bar with an alarming number of empty glasses in front of him, waiting for a friend who is late coming back from a job that clearly went wrong.

### Marshal Onyx Tran

Something about him reads as increasingly unstable beneath the iron composure — a soldier being slowly possessed by an ideology he thinks is his own certainty.

### Elder Vesper

### "Red Flag"

## KEY LOCATIONS

### The Still Gardens (Thessavar)
> A crescent-shaped private island, half-drowned, built on centuries of stacked sunken ruins — hedge-walled waterways threading between white stone garden terraces like a green maze rising out of black ocean. At its heart: a weathered formation of ancient stepped stone, moss-eaten, older than any record, its high terrace lit for a ceremony that doesn't know it's about to end. The whole island has the eerie beauty of Venice slowly losing an argument with the sea.

### The swan boats
> Gondola-style boats towed by biomechanical swans — pure white feathers over a segmented, faintly visible dark hydraulic understructure at the joints. Their necks move with unnervingly smooth, too-perfect mechanical precision; their eyes are wet, bloodshot, disturbingly organic against the artificial stillness of the rest of them. They glide without oars, without a driver, and leave absolutely no wake on the water — silent, synchronized, faintly wrong in a way guests have learned not to look at too closely.

### Belum Freeport
> A sprawling trade station patchworked together from centuries of retrofits — new neon signage bolted over pitted, centuries-old hull plating, scaffolding threading between mismatched architectural eras like scar tissue. The docks and promenade sit in the upper levels, bright, crowded, busy with Driftborn traffic and news screens; below, the undercity is a warren of dim maintenance corridors, steam vents, and territory nobody official governs. The whole station has the loud, functional ugliness of a port that has never once slowed down to be beautiful.

## FACTION VISUAL IDENTITY & SYMBOLOGY

*Use these to visually mark an NPC, ship, or location as belonging to a faction at a glance — the "tell" a player should learn to recognize on sight.*

**The Pale Substrate** — Symbol: a pale, minimalist circular emblem, almost a blank halo, cold and clinical. Palette: white, pale grey, sterile blue-white light. Visual tell: neural substrate case-scars at the skull/spine, wellness-monitor badges, spaces that are *too* clean and *too* quiet.

**The Aureole Synod** — Symbol: a radiant halo/corona, warm gold light around a dark center (the hidden sun you can't look at directly). Palette: warm gold, soft cream robes, candlelight. Visual tell: chapels that feel genuinely comforting; clergy who somehow always seem to know more than they should.

**Progenitor Combine** — Symbol: a stylized double-helix crossed with an assembly-line motif — biology as manufacturing. Palette: clinical white and steel, product-catalog color-coding by "grade" (security clones in grey, domestic clones in warmer tones). Visual tell: identical faces in groups, corporate badges, the specific uncanny of mass-produced people.

**The Hollow Covenant** — Symbol: a cracked ceremonial mask. Palette: split identity between the two factions — **Remembrance** in dark, imperial ceremonial reds and blacks, old-world military insignia; **Becoming** in looser, warmer earth tones, warrior-monk robes rather than uniforms. Visual tell: the mask motif appears on everything, cracked in a different place depending on which faction wears it.

**The Succession** — Symbol: a stylized eagle-and-star Mandate seal, imperial and dated on purpose. Palette: faded navy and tarnished gold, old-world military dress that hasn't updated its cut in three hundred years. Visual tell: everything is a little too formal, a little too pressed, like a costume of a government that no longer fully exists.

**The Argent Compact** — Symbol: a plain silver circle or mirror-disc — reflects everything equally, takes no side. Palette: silver, soft neutral greys, warm library wood tones. Visual tell: genuinely welcoming spaces, open doors, the calm of true neutral ground.

**The Penumbra** — Symbol: a crescent shadow at the edge of a bright disc — an eclipse mid-motion. Palette: dark charcoal and dim amber, half-lit spaces. Visual tell: cover identities that almost fit, agents who are always slightly out of focus in a crowd.

**The River Below** — Symbol: a breaking wave with a flame inside it. Palette: raw salvage-punk — rust, patched leather, hand-painted slogans, tattoo ink. Visual tell: young, scarred, loud aesthetics on deliberately loud ships; graffiti tags of the wave-and-flame symbol in dockside districts.

**The Driftborn** — Symbol: no single emblem — instead, ship names painted large and personal (*the Duskline, the Unburned Hour*) function as identity. Palette: practical, patched, lived-in — sun-faded work clothes, tool belts, cargo-hold clutter. Visual tell: a culture that decorates ships, not bodies; found-family energy, loud celebrations, close quarters.
