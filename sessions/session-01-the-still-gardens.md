# Session 1 — The Drowning of the Still Gardens

> *Thessavar. 3231. A world that is 96% ocean.*
> *The Still Gardens — the sacred island estate where the sector's powerful gather to carve up what sleeps beneath the waves.*

**Player characters:**
- **Enkh Zahli** — 70, blue-collar lifer turned criminal, cybernetic brain + right leg + left arm, owned by a debt to PALE. Here under a forged invitation to **assassinate Arbiter Senn**. Dressed for a retirement he never took: Hawaiian shirt, shorts, sandals.
- **West** (K7G2-87459-WST) — 18-month-old Kael-7 security clone, Grade 2, genetically compelled to obey legitimate authority. Part of Senn's three-clone detail. Here to **protect Arbiter Senn**.

**The engine of the session:** West is here to save her. Enkh is here to kill her. *Neither of them does it.* A third party — the **River Below** — tries to crack open the ancient vault beneath the ceremony and triggers a defense system that has slept for tens of thousands of years. Senn dies by accident. The island begins to drown. The two people sent to decide her fate become refugees, pulled out of the water by the very movement that doomed her — and end the night sharing a stolen berth on a freighter leaving the only world either of them has standing.

**Tone:** Brennan Lee Mulligan pacing. Beautiful, then catastrophic. Every beat earns the next with a **but** (complication) or a **therefore** (consequence) — never "and then." Read the *italic boxes* aloud or paraphrase; everything else is yours.

---

## GM Briefing — What Is Actually True (Players Don't Get This)

**The Lattice.** 400+ meters below Thessavar's surface sits a consciousness archive built by the **Architects**, a pre-human alien civilization that predates the Scream by tens of thousands of years. They did not leave bodies or weapons. They uploaded the entirety of their civilization — knowledge, memory, identity — into a crystalline lattice and went still. They are not dead. They are *stored*, and the archive is the only thing they have left. The defense system exists because destroying the archive destroys them, finally and forever.

**The layers of Thessavar (only a handful of living people know past Layer 1):**
1. *What everyone knows* — the ocean hides **pre-Scream human ruins**, and pre-Scream tech is worth a fortune in a post-Silence sector. The Concordance treaty is officially about excavation rights. This is why the elite came.
2. *What a few researchers know* — the pre-Scream ruins don't bottom out at the 400m thermocline. They were built **on top of something older**, and recent deep-dives past 600m breached a sealed zone no one had reached. That's when Senn and Tarek understood what was really down there.
3. *What almost no one knows* — the **Lattice vault**. Alien. The pre-Scream humans felt its passive resonance and built their sacred site directly above it without ever reaching it. The Still Gardens' weathered stone heart is the eroded cap of that site — and beneath it, an access shaft they sealed and forgot.

**The relic / the locket.** A fragment already extracted from the Lattice. It is a **paired Architect receiver** — designed to split into two halves, because the Architects ran on consensus and built their tools for two minds at once. The Lattice reads anyone who touches it and tries to *repair them to Architect standards*:
- **Enkh:** It reads PALE's flattening of his affect as **damage** and starts giving feeling back — grief, anger, the weight of stolen years. He didn't ask for this. He'd made peace with the numbness.
- **West:** It reads the genetic obedience as a **constraint on a mind it's trying to index** and gives West *awareness of the gap* between what they want and what they're compelled to do. The first crack of personhood.
- The halves stay faintly linked to the same broadcast. If both PCs wear a half, they may share fragments — the same impossible geometry at the edge of a dream. Entirely optional; it can sit unused in a pocket forever.

**The broadcast.** The breach wakes the Lattice. It begins transmitting on psionic frequencies across the sector — a search signal. Every faction with the right receivers is about to learn that *something woke up on Thessavar.* River Below rang a bell that cannot be un-rung, and they have no idea.

**Who set off the disaster.** A **River Below** diver, defying Veronika's explicit order, took a research submersible during the ceremony and tried to force the vault with a shaped charge. No key, no knowledge. The vault sealed around them — they die 600m down, no body, no grave. The defense system answers a physical assault on the archive with **geological force**: a pressurized column straight up the island's sealed stone heart.

**What each faction wants** (for your back pocket — see *Mysteries* at the end):
- **PALE** — arranged Enkh's contract to *delay* the treaty, not because it doesn't want the ruins but because it doesn't want the *Covenant* to have them first. A pre-Scream-surviving AI just learned a vastly older one exists. (Enkh must NOT learn this for several sessions.)
- **Hollow Covenant / Remembrance (Marshal Tran)** — thinks it's a weapon cache. Reckless. Wrong.
- **Hollow Covenant / Becoming (Elder Vesper)** — her whole philosophy *is* what the Lattice is. Tarek said trust her because she may be the only leader who understands it. West reports to her.
- **Progenitor Combine** — the Architects solved identity and memory across millennia. The Combine manufactures identity. The note warns they'll attempt **lineage mapping** — and West is a Combine product.
- **River Below** — Veronika's grassroots, Billy-Talent-mythologized counterculture. Robin Hood dreams, youthful disorganization. They overheard; they did not understand.

## The Geography (keep consistent with your in-app world notes)

- **Thessavar** — ocean world, 96% water. Human habitation clings to floating platform cities and the exposed peaks of drowned mountains.
- **Kaeldrift** (pop. ~80,000) — the main platform city, on a shallow shelf to the north. The working city. **Veronika's bar is here**, off the Concordance circuit — where Tarek and Senn met to talk freely.
- **The Still Gardens** — a private island estate ~40 minutes by water south of Kaeldrift. A natural rock formation built over with centuries of engineered hedges, formal garden terraces, and a maze of narrow waterways that connect the estate's sections like a living thing. Guests are ferried in and move between terraces by **swan boat** — long white craft poled by hand, because an engine is vulgar on water this old. At the estate's center: a weathered formation of stepped stone nobody can quite date, its high terrace the ceremonial summit. Nobody knows it's the cap of the access shaft.
- **The depths** — pre-Scream ruins begin around the 400m thermocline; the sealed alien zone (the Lattice vault) lies past 600m, reachable only by submersible.

# THE COLD OPEN

> *Run this before anything else. No character sheets out yet. The players do not know this man. They will.*

*Six hundred meters down, the water has no color. Tarek's dive-light is a small religion against all of it.*

*He is not supposed to be here. The research sub idles behind him, its hull ticking against pressure. In front of him: a wall that is not pre-Scream, not human, not anything the catalog has a word for. It is older than the ruins built on top of it the way a mountain is older than a footpath.*

*And it is awake. He can feel it the way you feel someone reading over your shoulder.*

Tarek touches the lattice. (You don't show the players what he sees. You show them his face — what *being indexed* does to a man.)

*He pulls his hand back like it burned. He is breathing too fast for someone standing still.*

He records a message. Sealed packet, addressed to his unit — **to West's unit.** Read it flat and quiet:

> **TAREK:** "If you're reading this, something went wrong. Trust Elder Vesper. Do not trust the relic."

*He seals it. He surfaces. He has one more thing to do before he disappears, and he will do it where the light is warmer and the people are beautiful and not one of them knows what is under their feet.*

*Then a sound in the dark behind him — a second engine in the water that should not be there.*

*Cut to black.*

# THE BEFORE — Two Flashbacks

> *These are the character introductions. Run them right after the cold open, before the boats. **Let the players act them** — don't narrate at the table. Each is one tight scene with one job and one planted echo that pays off tonight. Keep them short; do not let them sprawl. Then snap to the present.*

## Flashback A — West: Synthesis Prime (≈ six months ago)

*Caption: SYNTHESIS PRIME. LYRETH SYSTEM. CONDITIONING LEVEL 9.*

> *White floors. Calibrated acoustics that make your own breathing sound like a decision. You are not yet West. You are K7G2-87459, Grade 2, eleven weeks from deployment, and the world is very simple and very clean.*

Set West a final conditioning drill with the **instructor** (consistent, professional, never cruel — the Combine is good at what it does). A protectee scenario. West performs it correctly, because performing it correctly is the only thing that has ever felt like anything. Let the player feel **how good obedience is** — how it fits, how it quiets the noise, how being *for* something is the closest thing to peace they've been built to have.

End on the echo. The instructor, packing up, almost offhand:

> **INSTRUCTOR:** "Last thing. When the principal is gone — dead, taken, doesn't matter — you don't freeze. You fall in. You find the next legitimate authority and you fall in. A Kael-7 alone is a malfunction. You understand? *You always fall in.*"
> **K7G2-87459:** "I understand."

**The echo's job:** in Act III, when West stands frozen with a sealed package and chooses *not* to fall in, the whole table should hear this instructor's voice breaking.

> GM note: don't explain the echo now. Plant it and move on. If the player wants, let them establish their bond with **Nor** here — a glance, a shared drill — so Act III has something to cost them.

## Flashback B — Enkh: Kaeldrift, Veronika's bar (five days ago)

*Caption: KAELDRIFT. FIVE DAYS BEFORE THE CONCORDANCE.*

> *A working-city bar that smells like brine and cheap stim-coffee. The bartender keeps to herself and keeps your glass full. Across the table, a man in his forties is looking at you like he's trying to find someone he used to know.*

This is **Darius.** Let Enkh's player play the planning session — the forged invitation, the timing, the route off the island. Darius runs the logistics; he's done this with Enkh before, back when it was theft and smuggling, not *this.* Let the relationship breathe: Darius needles him about PALE, mentions the old Enkh, maybe mentions **Div** without meaning to. Let Enkh be, for one scene, a person someone has known for twenty years and grieves a little.

The bartender refilling their glasses, by the way, is **Veronika.** Neither of them notices her. She notices them. *(Pure dramatic irony for you — do not flag it.)*

End on the echo. Darius, quiet, not looking up from the map:

> **DARIUS:** "Fifteen grand. *Exactly* fifteen, down to the credit. Same as your balance." *(beat)* "Enkh. Nobody pays your number by accident. Somebody at PALE knows what you owe to the credit, and somebody's dangling the exact figure to make sure you say yes." *(he finally looks up)* "I'm not telling you not to. I'm telling you to notice."

Enkh can wave it off. **The echo's job:** when the contract evaporates and the client goes dark tonight, *this* is the conversation that comes back. The player will notice even if the character won't.

> GM note: this scene also quietly loads the campaign's engine — the contract, PALE's fingerprints, the debt, Darius as a living face instead of a comm voice. It's the more *plot*-necessary of the two flashbacks; if you're tight on time, run this one fuller and West's leaner.

*Snap forward. Five days pass in a cut. Now — the present, and the water.*

# ACT I — TWO BOATS ON A GOLDEN CANAL

*Goal: establish the world, both PCs in the present, and the single most important image of the night — the two of them passing each other before they ever meet.*

> *The guests come down from Kaeldrift by water, forty minutes south across an ocean that goes on past every horizon, until the Still Gardens rise out of the lagoon — terraces of pale blossom and black stone, hedges shaped over centuries, a maze of green canals threaded with long white swan boats. At the estate's center, half-swallowed by garden, a formation of weathered stepped stone older than anyone's records. Tonight its high terrace will hold the most important signature of the decade.*
>
> *Nobody on the water knows the stone is hollow. Nobody knows what's under it.*

**Run the two arrivals as a hard intercut.** Keep them apart. The players should feel the near-miss.

### West — on the water, on duty

*You are in the lead swan boat of Arbiter Senn's procession, gliding through the garden canals toward the stone terrace. Senn sits ahead of you in white. She has not looked at you, or at either of your unit, since you boarded. This is correct. You are not for looking at. You are for the eleven faces you have already flagged in the crowds along the canal — and the eleventh just turned away too quickly.*

- Let West feel the **unit** beside them — Nor and/or Ost, however the player set it up in the flashback. Establish the *we*, because Act III is going to take it away.
- The genetic obedience is **comfortable** right now — frame it as peace, not a cage. Senn's silence reads to West as status. *She is so far above us she does not need to see us. That is how we know she matters.*

### Enkh — on the water, going the other way

*You are in a hired swan boat threading the same canals, dressed like a man who retired last week — because that was the plan, before the accident, before PALE, before twenty years of paying for a brain you didn't ask for. The forged token says you belong here. The job says you'll leave it lighter by one diplomat and heavier by fifteen thousand credits — the exact number that clears the Lease. The exact number. Darius's voice is still in the back of your skull. So is the other thing in the back of your skull, the faint weight you've learned to ignore. Mostly.*

- Enkh is **casing**, not feeling. His flat affect is an asset in a room full of performance: he's the only one not acting.

### THE PASS

*The canal narrows where two hedges lean together over the water. Your boats meet there — one going up to the terrace, one crossing toward the estate. For three seconds you are close enough to pass a cup of water hand to hand.*

*An old man in a loud shirt. A security clone in grey. Neither of you knows the other. Neither of you will ever be strangers again after tonight — but you don't know that yet either.*

*Your boats pass. The canals carry you apart.*

> GM note: name no names, exchange no words. Maybe a half-second of eye contact — the clone clocks the old man's stillness; the old man clocks the security detail. File it and move on. The whole table should feel the door that just didn't open.

**Therefore** — both boats arrive at the heart of the Still Gardens, where the party has already begun.

# ACT II — THE CONCORDANCE

*Goal: let both PCs work the same party toward opposite goals, plant every payload, and hand West the package. This is the calm before. Make it gorgeous and make it tick.*

> *The central terrace is a garden grown on the roof of the world — pale blossoms in black stone planters, light strung like captured stars, the whole sector's money in one place pretending it came for the flowers. Servers move through the crowd. At the edge, a temporary bar — and behind it, a woman with steady hands who refills glasses and listens to everything.*

That woman is **Veronika.** She came down from Kaeldrift to run River Below's surface operation. She is invisible tonight, exactly as she likes.

### West's thread: the impossible order

Tarek finds West at the edge of the detail. Warm, practiced, a little too fast — a man with somewhere to be.

> **TAREK:** "Kael-7. Good. You'll do." *(He presses a sealed package into West's hands — small, heavy, warm from his pocket.)* "This goes to Arbiter Senn. After the ceremony, not before — she can't be distracted now, and it's safer with you than with me. You don't open it. You don't scan it. You give it to her, after. Understood?"

- He's gone before West can flag him. **But** the order is clean and legitimate, so it settles into West like all orders do: a small, certain weight. *Give this to Senn after the ceremony.* Easy. Safe.
- **GM seed:** that word *after* is a trap the universe is setting. There will be no after for Senn.

### Enkh's thread: the angles

*Senn is across the terrace, ringed by handlers and two clones in grey — and you realize the third clone, the one the broad-shouldered man just spoke to, is the one from the boat. Small island.*

- Let Enkh **work the problem**: Senn's movements, sightlines, the route off the estate. He's a thief and a smuggler by trade — assassination is a new low and he knows it. Give him a beat to feel that, or to refuse to.
- **Complication (but):** Senn is *never* alone. The detail is too good. The clean kill isn't here yet. He'll have to wait for the ceremony, when every eye is forward. *Therefore he's still waiting when the world ends.*

### Optional connective tissue (use what the table bites on)

- **The relic is the worst-kept secret at the party.** A Covenant aide shows select guests a flat case — *proof of what's beneath the waves.* (This is a **decoy**. The *real* fragment is the sealed package in West's hands. Let the players assume the case is the prize. It isn't.)
- **Tran's representative** works the room against the treaty — too loud, too sure. Plants that the Covenant is *not* united.
- **Darius pings Enkh** mid-party — a voice in his ear from Kaeldrift. "You're really there?" Thirty seconds of being someone's friend. He hates PALE and says so again. Bank it.
- **Veronika clocks both PCs** without either noticing. A bartender remembers faces — and these two she's seen before, though only she knows it.

**Therefore** — a bell, a hush, a call to gather. The Arbiter ascends the ceremonial terrace atop the old stone. Everyone turns to watch the most important signature of the decade.

*Six hundred meters down, in the dark Tarek surfaced from, a second engine cuts out. A young River Below diver braces a shaped charge against a wall older than the human species — and does not hear Veronika's voice in the back of their head saying wait.*

# ACT III — THE GEYSER

*Goal: kill Senn beyond all doubt, wake the defense system, drown the island. This is the hinge. Go fast and go loud. Short sentences. Don't pause for rolls that don't matter — narrate the disaster and let the PCs react inside it.*

> *Senn lifts the stylus. The treaty hangs in the air on a projected sheet of light. She begins to speak — about partnership, about the future buried beneath the waves, about—*

**She never finishes the sentence.**

> *The stone screams.*
>
> *Not a sound — a pressure, felt in the teeth and the water and the centuries of stacked rock. The sacred geometry under the terrace was never decoration. It was a seal. And the thing it sealed has just decided it is under attack.*

Beat. Then the column.

> *A pillar of water comes up through the heart of the old stone — through the access shaft no living person knew was a shaft — with the force of an ocean that has been waiting tens of thousands of years to be let in the wrong direction. The terrace does not collapse.*
>
> *It launches.*
>
> *Arbiter Senn goes up. Stylus still in her hand. Up past the strung lights, up past the hedges, up until she is a white mark against the dark and then not even that. The column holds her there for one impossible second — a woman a thousand feet over her own treaty — and then the water lets go.*
>
> *She comes down into the lagoon. There is no body to recover. There is no question to ask. Everyone saw it. Both of you saw it.*
>
> *West — the person you exist to protect just died in a way you could not have stopped, prevented, or predicted.*
>
> *Enkh — your target is dead, your contract is void, and you did not pull the trigger. The fifteen thousand credits just walked off the edge of the world without you.*

**Now the cascade. Hit these fast, in any order the table demands — but/therefore the whole way down:**

1. **The defense system doesn't stop.** It's *pulling the vault deeper* to protect it, and the rock the island stands on goes with it. The lagoon drains in some places and surges in others. The drowned mountain under the Still Gardens exhales. **Therefore** the island begins, terrace by terrace, to go under.
2. **The EMP.** A pulse rolls out from the stone's core. Comms die across the estate. **Enkh** — your substrate stutters. For one second the back-of-the-skull weight you've carried for ten years goes *silent.* PALE is not watching. You didn't know how loud the watching was until it stopped. Then it flickers back, or doesn't yet — your call to sit with. *(Mechanically: brief disorientation, no lasting damage. The beat is emotional, not a stat hit.)*
3. **The locket wakes.** The sealed package in **West's** hands grows warm — properly warm, alive-warm. *(It keeps until West chooses to open it. Don't force it. But a dead principal + an impossible order + a warmth that feels like being seen is a lot to hold while the floor tilts.)*
4. **The unit breaks.** Hit West hard and clean:
   - *One of your unit is gone* — caught when the terrace launched, or under the first slab of falling stone. No grey shape comes up out of the water.
   - *The other is already moving* — herding the Covenant delegation toward the evac boats, executing the protection protocol on the nearest legitimate authority, because that is what you *do* when the principal is dead and the procedure is still clear. They call your designation once, expecting you to fall in. *(This is the instructor's voice from the flashback, made flesh: "You always fall in.")*
   - **But** there is no principal to protect, and a sealed package addressed to a dead woman, and a warmth against your palm that feels like being *seen* — and for the first time in eighteen months of existence, the order and the want are not the same thing. **Therefore West has to choose.** Fall in with the unit and the protocol — or don't. Hold that silence as long as the player needs. It's the most important second of their life.
     > *(Which clone dies and which leaves is yours — but the cruelest, cleanest version kills the one West bonded with in the flashback and leaves the stranger to say "fall in." Flip it if the player would rather their friend live to walk away.)*
5. **The elite flee.** Money does not die gracefully. Guests storm the swan boats and the private transports; sacred ground is just ground now, and sinking ground at that. The way out is a crush. **Therefore** neither PC is getting off this island by the front canals.

**And then a hand.**

> *A boat you didn't book pulls alongside a flooding lower terrace — a flat-bottomed canal runner, fast and ugly and perfect, poled by a woman you last saw refilling glasses. She is not panicking. She is the only person on this entire drowning island who is not panicking.*

> **VERONIKA:** "You two. Loud shirt, grey suit. You can stand here and sink with the rich folks, or you can get in the boat. I know these channels — I can run us out through the garden maze and the old ruins while they're all fighting over the front canal." *(A breath. Something cracks under it — guilt she won't name yet.)* "I didn't want this. Get in. Ask me later."

**The choice that ends the act:** get in the boat. (They will. If a player balks, the island obliges — a terrace gives way, the front canal becomes a deathtrap, the only hand still offered is hers.) An assassin, a bodyguard, and the woman whose movement just drowned an island, in one stolen boat, threading the flooding maze while the Still Gardens go under behind them.

> *Behind you, the old stone is going under one ancient layer at a time, centuries of stubborn garden finally losing its argument with the sea. Somewhere six hundred meters down, in a vault no one can reach now, a sealed civilization finishes waking up and begins, for the first time in tens of thousands of years, to call out across the stars.*
>
> *Nobody with the ears to hear it is listening.*
>
> *Yet.*

# THE ESCAPE / SESSION CLOSE

*Goal: down-shift, let the three strangers breathe, open the threads that pull into Session 2.*

Veronika runs them out through the garden maze and the submerged ruins offshore, into open water, toward Kaeldrift's freight moorings — where a merchant freighter is loading to leave a planet that just lost its sacred heart. A berth can be bought, stolen, or talked onto. Now the quiet beats land:

- **West and the package.** With Senn dead, the order *give this to her after the ceremony* has no path to completion — possibly the first impossible order of West's life, and the instructor's *you always fall in* has no one left to fall in behind. What do they do? Likely: open it. Let them. Inside: the **locket fragment** and **Tarek's note.** Hand the note over (full text below). Let it detonate quietly.
- **Enkh's dead contract.** His client is dark — EMP, or the line was always one-way. The target's dead and he didn't do it. *Does he get paid? Does whoever hired him know it wasn't him? Who hired him?* And under all of it, Darius's voice: *nobody pays your number by accident.* That itch is his road into the campaign.
- **The locket, offered.** Now or later, the fragment can **split in two.** If/when each PC touches their half: Enkh gets a thread of *feeling* back (start small and unwelcome — grief, or the kid Div's face, or just the fact that the loud shirt was for a life he never got to live). West gets the *gap* — the first awareness that what they want and what they're built to do are two different things. **Totally optional.** It can ride in a pocket, unused, indefinitely. Make sure they know that — and that Tarek's note explicitly tells them *not* to divide it.
- **Who is Veronika?** She'll give them The Saint before she gives them Veronika. She owes them an explanation and isn't ready for the whole truth — she doesn't know what was *really* down there, only that her own diver went after it against her word and it cost everything. Let her be guilty, capable, and holding back.

### Tarek's Note (hand to West)

> *Senn —*
> *The lattice is intact and it is not passive. It indexed me when I was near the primary node. I think it reached you too, through the fragment. It is looking for receivers and I do not think it has found enough of them.*
> *Becoming has to know before Remembrance does. If Tran gets there first he will treat it like an armory and he will be wrong in the worst possible way.*
> *Do not let the fragment be divided or copied. If the Combine understands what it carries they will attempt lineage mapping, and I have seen what they do with a key.*
> *PALE has read access to our channel. Do not answer the usual way. Come alone. Burn this.*
> *— T*

**Why it lands on West specifically:**
- *"the Combine will attempt lineage mapping"* — West **is** a Combine product. The thing in their hands is something their makers would weaponize.
- *"PALE has read access"* — West doesn't know what PALE is. **Enkh does.** First thread that ties the two PCs' backstories into one rope.
- *"It reached you too… I think"* — Senn touched the fragment. Senn is dead. Did the Lattice log her? Keep her?
- *"Do not let the fragment be divided"* — and the players are very likely about to divide it. Dramatic irony: the rulebook tells them not to do the thing that gives them their powers. Let them choose with eyes open.
- *"Burn this."* — one more impossible instruction for a clone who doesn't destroy things without authorization. Their authority is dead. Whose order do they follow now?

**End the session on a question, not a resolution.** Suggested final image and line — the three of them aboard, Thessavar shrinking behind them:

> *Three people who were never supposed to be in the same boat. One sent to kill her. One sent to save her. One whose people drowned her by accident reaching for something they didn't understand. The Arbiter is dead and not one of you did it on purpose. The island is gone. And the only thing any of you is sure of is that whatever just happened on Thessavar, the rest of the sector is going to want to know who to blame.*
>
> *Veronika doesn't look back at the water. "So," she says. "Where do people like us go, when there's nowhere on that rock left to stand?"*

*Roll credits.*

# Between Sessions — Faction Turn

Run this as the SWN faction turn (GM Turn screen). The throughline: **nobody who matters knows what really happened, and everybody assumes it was someone else.**

- **Hollow Covenant fractures.** Senn is dead mid-signature; the treaty is ash. **Remembrance (Tran)** blames Becoming's soft diplomacy for exposing them. **Becoming (Vesper)** knows more than she'll say and goes quiet and careful. The fragile internal truce snaps.
- **Everyone blames everyone.** Outside powers blame the Covenant for waking something. The Covenant blames outside actors. River Below cells across the sector hear that *their* movement was at Thessavar and split — some radicalize ("we woke a god, good"), some recoil in horror.
- **The broadcast spreads.** Factions with the right receivers start logging the signal from Thessavar. None of them understand it. All of them want it. **PALE** understands the most and says the least.
