# Session 3 — Three Doors, One Night

> *Belum Freeport doesn't slow down for anyone. Three doors are already open. The trick tonight isn't finding a way in — it's not letting all three close on you at once.*

**Player characters:**
- **Enkh Zahli** — Level 2. Broke. The 15,000cr payoff is dead; Coyle's decline is loaded and waiting the moment he walks in.
- **West** (K7G2-87459-WST) — Level 2. Carrying a warm, unsplit locket and Tarek's letter, still secret from everyone, including Enkh. Ownership contested three ways.

**The engine of the session:** All three doors from the end of Session 2 — Veronika's cargo run, Coyle's decline, Sister Halex's ask — get answered in one fast night, back to back, no dead air. Two of them are information and pressure. One of them is a fight, and the fight is not what it looks like: somebody already knows something is warm in West's pocket, and they came to take it. The night ends with the locket still theirs, a body on the ground that won't explain itself, and a legal team stepping onto the gangway before anyone's caught their breath.

**Tone:** No idle stretches. Every door opens fast and closes hard. This is the session that punishes lingering — keep the clock audible from the cold open on.

---

## GM Briefing — What Is Actually True (Players Don't Get This)

**Ost and Tarek.** Both captured by **PALE**, not AURIS — PALE had a survey team logging anomalies at the primary node when Tarek touched the Lattice, and its own Kaeldrift disaster-relief operation picked him up clean as a "shock victim." He's held at a PALE Reconstruction & Wellness relief station, being quietly leashed the way Enkh is. Under soft, gentle, total pressure he's confirmed the locket is real and that it indexed at least one member of West's unit — but he has stayed deliberately vague about *which one*, his one remaining act of loyalty to West. **None of this reaches the table tonight.** It's load-bearing for later, not tonight's reveal.

**Who's coming for the locket.** Rhax's debt syndicate (introduced in the Session 2 prep as the muscle backing Vess Okaro's creditors) has an intake category for "PALE-job" cyberware and, separately, has been quietly buying the debts of Concordance witnesses. Word has now reached them — through a loose-lipped relief-station scanner tech, several hands removed from anything anyone can trace — that "one of the security clones off Thessavar runs warm." That's all they have: a rumor, not a name, not a face. Tonight they send freelance muscle to find out during the one moment West is exposed: the Veronika crate run. **This is NOT confirmed as PALE tonight and should never be stated as fact at the table.** It's deniable, sourced third-hand, exactly the kind of lead a debt syndicate chases on spec.

**Sister Halex.** Remembrance by faction, personally loyal to the late Arbiter Senn. Tonight she tells the party what Senn was actually trying to accomplish with the Concordance treaty — not "carve up a weapon," but negotiate real *shared* access to the ruins before either Covenant wing or the Combine could lock it down alone. Senn's death didn't just kill a person; it killed the one plan that wasn't a land grab. Halex also finally names the split inside her own faction: Marshal Tran's wing (the Radiance) wants the ruins as leverage; Halex's wing wants Senn's actual treaty honored. She is one sister, alone, doing this without her chain of command's blessing.

**Coyle.** Still afraid of his client. Still has no idea (and won't learn tonight) that the client is PALE. His decline scene is unchanged from Session 2 prep — run it fast, it's a gut-punch, not a mystery to solve tonight.

**The repo team.** Lands THIS session, not banked. They serve West's writ on the docks as the closing beat — the hard clock the whole night has been racing.

# COLD OPEN — THREE DOORS, ALREADY OPEN

*S2 ended standing at the docks with all three offers live. Don't replay the Tableau or the arrival — already played.*

> *"You're standing on the Belum docks. Coyle's sign is two streets up. Veronika's waiting on the Duskline with a job. Sister Halex is still there, patient, watching you decide."*

- Let the players pick the order out loud — don't force it. Whatever they pick first, telegraph that the other two are still ticking (Veronika: "the crates move tonight or not at all." Coyle: office closes at dusk. Halex: Emmerin's been held four days already, she's scared for him).
- Fast beat, not a scene: whichever door they *don't* pick first should visibly still be waiting when they're done with the first — a face in the crowd, a runner catching up to them, a comm ping.

# DOOR — COYLE (small, fast, no combat)

*Run this one lean. It's a gut-punch, not a puzzle.*

- Coyle slides the declination across the table. *"Cause of death doesn't match the commissioned work."* Sympathetic, precise, afraid. Pressed hard: *"I checked the routing twice and then stopped checking. You understand? I stopped checking."*
- Do not explain how the client knew. Let it hang.
- Then the small job: recover a pawned pretech gyroscope from Vess Okaro, 2,000cr.
- Okaro is terrified, not hostile — she owes Rhax's syndicate, and they've been quietly buying up Concordance witnesses' debts. She surrenders the gyro if the PCs help her vanish before the next collection visit. **Resolve this by Talk, not combat, if at all possible** — this door exists to plant the Rhax-syndicate thread that pays off in the Veronika fight, not to burn table time on a second firefight.
- ✎ Talk DC 10 — Okaro surrenders the gyro, no fight. ✎ Notice DC 8 — she's scared of someone worse than the PCs.
- **Payoff:** 2,000cr against a 15,000cr debt. Coyle owes a favor. Enkh clocks that Okaro's situation is his, five years further down.

# DOOR — SISTER HALEX (Senn's true goal, the nun split — no real combat)

*This is where the players learn what the campaign is actually about.*

- Halex, tired and direct: Brother Emmerin, four days missing in the undercity, carrying a sealed dispatch for Elder Vesper. She's asking, not ordering — her own faction is watching her since Senn died.
- **Say this plainly, in her voice, this session:** *"Senn wasn't trying to seal anything away, and she wasn't trying to hand it to anyone either. She was trying to build a treaty where nobody got to own it alone — not the Combine, not us, not the Succession. She thought that was the only way it didn't get someone killed. She was right. It got HER killed instead."*
- **The nun split, named out loud:** *"I answer to Remembrance. I answered to HER. Marshal Tran's people are calling the ruins a weapon now that she's gone, and half my own order is starting to agree with him. I don't. That's not doctrine, that's just — grief looking for a fight. I'm doing this without anyone's blessing."*
- The Molt gang holds Emmerin for ransom they don't understand the value of. **Resolve fast** — a short parley (Talk DC 10: 500cr or a favor) should be enough to avoid or shorten a fight. If it goes loud, use the prepped Molt statline but let it break early (morale 6) — this isn't the session's combat set piece.
- Emmerin's dispatch: Senn's own notes confirm BOTH Covenant factions are hunting Kael-7 survivors — the Combine's lien and the Covenant's auction claim both stand, unresolved. Whether West reads the sealed dispatch is a real character beat.
- **Payoff:** passage toward Elder Vesper. West learns not every Remembrance sister is a zealot — and hears, for the first time from someone who knew her, what Senn actually wanted.

---

# DOOR — VERONIKA'S RUN (the session's one combat — someone comes for the locket)

*Protect time for this one. Everything else was a fuse; this is where it goes off.*

- Same setup as prepped: night crate transfer, Duskline to an outer moorage warehouse. "Medicine. Mostly." (True — meds and forged refugee IDs, River Below's real work.)
- Mid-transfer, the ambush hits — but reflavor it: this is **the Latch**, freelance muscle working a rumor, not Rustwater dock pirates working a stolen manifest. Three or four operators, professional, quiet entry, and they are not interested in the crates.
- **The tell:** one of them has a handheld scanner and is visibly checking it against each PC as the fight opens — they're triangulating, not looting. If a PC clocks this (✎ Notice DC 10), it should read as deeply wrong before anyone understands why.
- **The grab:** at some point mid-fight, one Latch operator breaks off and goes specifically for West — a called shot, a grapple, a hand reaching for the pocket where the locket rides warm. This is the scene's centerpiece: make the players *feel* someone reaching for the one thing West hasn't told anyone about.
- **Combat:** 3 Latch operators [HP 6, AC 13, +2, 1d8] + the scanner-runner [HP 5, AC 12, +1, 1d6, breaks off to grab rather than fight]. Crates as cover. They are better drilled than the Rustwater crew ever were — no wasted shots, no bravado.
- ✎ Notice DC 10 — the scanner, the triangulation, the fact these aren't casing crates. ✎ Exert or Punch DC 10 — break the grab before the locket's taken.
- **If the locket is actually grabbed:** don't let it leave the scene. Let it be *dropped, contested, nearly lost* — the fear of losing it should land harder than actually losing it would. This is the trap payoff (`locketState.theTrap`) working early: for one terrifying beat, West gets a taste of what it feels like without it.
- **The last operator down** (talked down, dying, or captured — GM's call which) says one line before going silent, half to itself: *"...she's not the only one who woke up warm."* Don't explain it. Don't confirm PALE. Let it sit as exactly what it is — a scrap of someone else's intel, not a reveal.
- **Payoff:** Veronika's trust deepens (she starts answering some questions straight). The players now know, hard and immediate, that someone out there already suspects what West is carrying — and that it isn't just paranoia anymore.

# SESSION CLOSE — THE GANGWAY

*Cut to this the moment the Veronika fight ends. No decompression scene first.*

> *"You're still catching your breath when you see them at the end of the gangway. Grey suits. Unhurried. One of them is holding a data-slate up like it's already been read into the record."*

- The Combine repo team serves West's writ, right there, right now — calm, legal, absolutely certain of themselves. They are not here to fight. They are here because the paperwork says they've already won.
- End on the standoff, unresolved. Roll credits on whichever PC speaks first.

## GM Quick-Reference — The But/Therefore Spine

1. Three doors open at once on the docks — **but** the players have to pick an order — **therefore** whichever they leave for last is the one that's had all night to go wrong.
2. Coyle's decline lands clean — **but** he can't explain how the client knew — **therefore** the mystery of "who's reading Enkh" gets one degree colder and stranger.
3. Halex finally says what Senn was really trying to do — **but** it's already too late to matter to Senn — **therefore** the players inherit a plan that only makes sense once its architect is dead.
4. Veronika's crate run looks like the same job as always — **but** someone's scanning for a specific PC, not the cargo — **therefore** the players learn, in real time, that West's secret isn't secret to everyone anymore.
5. The Latch grab the locket for one terrible beat — **but** West gets it back — **therefore** the players feel exactly what losing it would cost, without it actually costing anything yet.
6. The dying operator drops one unexplained line — **but** nobody confirms who sent them — **therefore** the mystery widens instead of closing, right as the repo team closes the night on a different front entirely.

## If They Go Off-Script (Safety Net)

- **Skip a door entirely:** fine — Coyle and Halex are both compressible to a few lines if time is short. Protect the Veronika combat above everything else; it's the one beat that can't be summarized after the fact.
- **They ask the Latch operator direct questions before he dies:** he genuinely doesn't know who hired the job past a cutout — give up the "she's not the only one who woke up warm" line and nothing more concrete. He's telling the truth; he just doesn't have more truth to give.
- **They try to fight the repo team:** they don't escalate first — it's legal-first pressure, not a firefight. Let a PC choose to make it violent if they want; the team will not.
- **West tells Enkh about the locket mid-session:** let it happen. It's overdue and it changes nothing about the plot machinery — Enkh still has nothing in his own logs about it, so PALE's suspicion stays exactly as uncertain as before.
